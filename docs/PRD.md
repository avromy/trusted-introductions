# Product Requirements Document

## Product

Trusted Introductions is a private, invite-only Jewish community job-helping platform where members help job seekers through warm introductions, network sharing, guidance, and tracked outcomes.

## Core Loop

Invite → Onboard → Match → Help → Track Outcome

## Non-Goals

- Not a public job board.
- Not an applicant tracking system.
- Not a recruiting agency workflow tool.
- Not a resume-scraping or bulk outreach product.
- Not a replacement for human endorsement.

## Primary Users

### Job Seeker

A trusted community member seeking job-related help, warm introductions, network access, advice, or role discovery.

### Helper

A trusted community member willing to make introductions, share network access, advise, review resumes, or refer candidates when appropriate.

### Community Steward

A trusted operator responsible for invitations, safety, quality, matching review, feature flags, and outcome health.

## Jobs To Be Done

- As a job seeker, I want to explain who I am and what help I need without exposing everything publicly.
- As a helper, I want to understand why I am being asked to help and decide how much access to share.
- As a steward, I want to preserve trust, monitor outcomes, and prevent misuse.

## MVP Scope

- Invite-only account creation.
- One trusted identity per person.
- Profile with contribution modes and privacy preferences.
- Job seeker request intake.
- Helper access and willingness signals.
- Explainable match suggestions.
- Human-reviewed introduction workflow.
- Outcome tracking.

## Success Metrics

- Invite acceptance rate.
- Completed onboarding rate.
- Qualified match acceptance rate.
- Introduction completion rate.
- Helpful outcome rate.
- Time from request to first useful help.
- Privacy incident count.

## Product Principles

- Trust over scale.
- Human before résumé.
- Match people to trusted access, not jobs.
- Preserve the human bridge.
- Explain every recommendation.
- Measure outcomes, not activity.
- Privacy settings override convenience.
- AI must not write the helper's personal endorsement.
